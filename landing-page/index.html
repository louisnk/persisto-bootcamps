Democratize Web Application Development


Mentor high-school youth in Tbilisi, Georgia September 16, 2017 - October 15, 2017

All Expenses Paid

Transformation through Education


Anyone can learn to code, sometimes it just takes a little help to get started. Online resources have done an incredible job making the content available, now we're partnering with the Georgian government to bring it the last mile.

Join us as we help a group of secondary school students (aged 13-17) learn to creatively problem solve on a team while learning to build simple web applications using Javascript, HTML, and CSS.


Get Involved

What's the deal?

4 Weeks

All expenses paid to come hang out at harvest time in beautiful Tbilisi, Georgia. Enjoy an 8,000 year old wine culture, unique cuisine, and a lifelong impact on the next generation.

30 Kids

30 kids aged 13-17, all eager to explore and learn. They'll have varying and limited exposure to desktop computers, but plenty of curiosity and experience playing with smartphones.

Unlimited learning

Kids are like sponges, and their creativity often challenges us to understand problems in new ways. We'll help unlock that creativity to build interesting things while they learn to code.


Put me in, coach!

Tbilisi is the capital and largest city of Georgia, with 1.5 million residents.

In a collaboration with the Georgian authorities, we will be utilizing state of the art equipment and facilities for the duration of the program.

Georgia is known as the heart of the Caucasus, with UNESCO World Heritage sites, rich food, and wine to reward those travelers who take the road less traveled.

Explore the local culture - In 2001, UNESCO declared the dancing traditions of Georgia as 'Masterpieces of the Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity'.

Persisto was founded by digital nomads. Together, we can provide students around the world with access to the same opportunities that have been afforded to us.

We are working with high school students, aged 13-17. It is urgent to shape the young minds of today to address the challenges of tomorrow.

Details

All expenses paid

Yep, you read that right. Nobody pays a penny to participate - mentors or students. Flights, food, (private) room, and curated experiences will all be covered for your 4 week stay.

(Stipend may be available for uniquely qualified mentors)

Leadership experience

Nervous about public speaking? Uncertain about that new leadership role? This is the perfect opportunity to overcome your fears and flex your mentor muscles with a forgiving audience.

5:1 student-mentor ratio

They'll be an eager bunch with a lot to learn, so we want to make sure everyone gets the mentorship they need.

Social impact

Where else can you have a bigger impact on the future of some kids in a 4 week period?
